The term "Japanese domestic market" ("JDM") refers to Japan's home market for vehicles and vehicle parts.[1] Japanese owners contend with a strict motor vehicle inspection and grey markets. The average age of JDM cars is 8.7 years, ranking 9th in a survey of 30 of the top 50 countries by gross domestic product.[2] According to the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile, a car in Japan travels a yearly average of over only 9,300 kilometres (5,800 mi), less than half the U.S. average of 19,200 kilometres (11,900 mi).



Japanese domestic market vehicles may differ greatly from the cars that Japanese manufacturers build for export and vehicles derived from the same platforms built in other countries. The Japanese car owner looks more toward innovation than long-term ownership which forces Japanese carmakers to refine new technologies and designs first in domestic vehicles. For instance, the 2003 Honda Inspire featured the first application of Honda's Variable Cylinder Management.

Nissan Skyline GT-R R34

Legendary for its RB26DETT engine, advanced all-wheel-drive system, and dominating performance in motorsports. The R34 GT-R is a symbol of Japanese engineering prowess and precision.

Toyota Supra MKIV

Powered by the 2JZ-GTE inline-6 engine, the Supra MKIV is known for its robust engine, rear-wheel-drive layout, and striking design. It's a favorite among tuners and has a strong presence in the enthusiast community.

Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI

Part of the Evolution series, the Evo VI features a turbocharged 4G63 engine, all-wheel-drive system, and aggressive rally-inspired styling. It's celebrated for its sharp handling and rally pedigree.

Subaru Impreza WRX STI (GC8)

The GC8 WRX STI boasts a turbocharged boxer engine, symmetrical all-wheel-drive, and a distinctive hood scoop. It's renowned for its rally success and is cherished by enthusiasts for its performance and versatility.

Mazda RX-7 FD

The RX-7 FD is powered by the iconic twin-turbocharged 13B rotary engine, offering a unique driving experience with its lightweight chassis and balanced handling. It's admired for its sleek design and rotary engine technology.

Honda NSX (First Generation)

Known for its mid-engine layout and lightweight aluminum construction, the NSX features a high-revving V6 engine and precise handling. It redefined the supercar segment with its reliability and everyday usability.
